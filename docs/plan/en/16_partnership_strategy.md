# WKU Software Crew Project - Partnership Strategy

## 1. Partnership Overview

### 1.1 Partnership Goals
- Expand user base through university partnerships
- Secure enterprise revenue (B2B)
- Access talent pipeline via corporate partnerships
- Leverage technology partnerships for infrastructure

### 1.2 Partnership Types
**University Partnerships**: Primary focus, drives user acquisition  
**Corporate Partnerships**: Recruitment, sponsorship, revenue  
**Technology Partnerships**: Infrastructure discounts, co-marketing

## 2. University Partnerships

### 2.1 Target Universities
**Year 1**: 3 universities in Jeollabuk-do  
**Year 2**: 10 universities nationally  
**Year 3**: 20+ universities

**Priority List (Year 1)**:
1. Wonkwang University (home university)
2. Jeonbuk National University
3. Kunsan National University

### 2.2 Partnership Models

**Model 1: Free Access Program**
- Free platform access for all CS students
- University promotes platform
- WKU gets user base, feedback
- **Revenue**: ₩0 (user acquisition)

**Model 2: Institutional License**
- University pays ₩10M-₩50M/year
- Unlimited student access
- Custom branding, dedicated support
- Integration with university LMS
- **Revenue**: ₩10M-₩50M/year per university

**Model 3: Co-Development**
- Joint curriculum development
- University provides instructors
- Revenue sharing (80% WKU, 20% University)
- **Revenue**: Variable

### 2.3 Partnership Benefits

**For Universities**:
- Modern learning platform (no development cost)
- Industry-relevant curriculum
- Student engagement and retention
- Job placement data
- Reduced infrastructure costs

**For WKU Software Crew**:
- Guaranteed user base
- Credibility and social proof
- Content creation support (professors)
- Recruitment pipeline (hire graduates)
- B2B revenue

### 2.4 Partnership Process

**Phase 1: Outreach** (Week 1-2)
- Identify decision makers (Dean, Department Head)
- Cold email + LinkedIn outreach
- Request introductory meeting

**Phase 2: Discovery** (Week 3-4)
- 30-minute intro call
- Understand university needs
- Present platform demo

**Phase 3: Pilot** (Month 2-4)
- Free 3-month pilot with 50-100 students
- Gather feedback, iterate
- Track engagement metrics

**Phase 4: Negotiation** (Month 5-6)
- Present pilot results
- Propose institutional license (₩10M-₩50M/year)
- Negotiate terms (pricing, contract length)

**Phase 5: Contract** (Month 7)
- Legal review
- Signed agreement
- Onboarding and training

**Timeline**: 6-7 months from first contact to signed contract

## 3. Corporate Partnerships

### 3.1 Target Corporates
**Tech Companies** (Samsung, Naver, Kakao):
- Recruiting partnerships
- Sponsorship (courses, challenges)

**Startups** (Korean unicorns):
- Internship programs
- Mentoring for students

**Educational Companies** (Megastudy, Visang):
- Content partnerships
- Distribution

### 3.2 Partnership Types

**Type 1: Recruitment Partnership** (₩5M-₩20M/year)
- Exclusive job board access
- Resume database
- Sponsored coding challenges
- Campus recruiting events

**Type 2: Sponsored Content** (₩2M-₩10M/campaign)
- Company-branded courses (e.g., "Web Dev with Naver Cloud")
- Tech talks and webinars
- Logo placement on platform
- Newsletter sponsorship

**Type 3: Internship Pipeline** (₩10M-₩50M/year)
- Pre-screened candidates
- Customized training program
- Exclusive access to top students
- Trial-to-hire model

### 3.3 Partnership Benefits

**For Corporates**:
- Access to vetted talent
- Employer branding
- Reduced recruiting costs
- Early engagement with students

**For WKU Software Crew**:
- Revenue diversification
- Corporate credibility
- Job placement for students (value-add)
- Industry connections
