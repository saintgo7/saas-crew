## 2. Market Segmentation (STP)

### 2.1 Segmentation

| Segment | Size | Characteristics | Priority |
|---------|------|-----------------|----------|
| **Wonkwang CS Students** | 500 | Motivated, local, early adopters | Primary |
| **Jeollabuk-do University Students** | 5,000 | Regional, varied majors | Secondary |
| **Self-learners (National)** | 50,000 | Online learners, career switchers | Tertiary |
| **Universities (B2B)** | 50 | Need integrated learning platforms | Primary |
| **Corporations (B2B)** | 200 | Recruitment, upskilling needs | Secondary |

### 2.2 Targeting

**Primary Target (Year 1)**: Wonkwang University students + 2-3 regional universities

**Why**:
- Local market is easier to penetrate
- Word-of-mouth spreads faster in universities
- Can iterate based on feedback quickly
- Lower customer acquisition cost

### 2.3 Positioning

**Positioning Statement**:
> "For university students who want to learn software development and launch startups, WKU Software Crew is an all-in-one platform that provides free learning, a cloud IDE, and startup support, unlike expensive online courses or fragmented tools."

**Key Differentiators**:
- University-focused (not generic)
- All-in-one (LMS + IDE + Deployment + Community)
- Free tier (accessible to all students)
- Local presence and support (Korean language, local mentors)
- Startup incubation (not just coding)

## 3. Marketing Mix (4Ps)

### 3.1 Product

**Core Offering**:
- Free LMS with Junior Class courses
- Cloud IDE with deployment
- Community Q&A forum
- Mentoring and startup support (premium)

**Packaging**:
- Free: Junior Class, basic IDE
- Premium (₩9,900/mo): Senior Class, advanced features
- Pro (₩29,900/mo): Master Class, 1:1 mentoring, unlimited deployments
- Enterprise (Custom): University licenses

### 3.2 Price

**Pricing Strategy**: Freemium

**Psychological Pricing**:
- ₩9,900 (not ₩10,000) - feels cheaper
- ₩29,900 (not ₩30,000) - psychological threshold

**Discounts**:
- Early bird: 50% off first 100 users
- Student verification: 20% off (with .edu email)
- Annual plans: 16% discount (2 months free)
- Referral: 1 month free for both parties

### 3.3 Place (Distribution)

**Channels**:
- Website (wkucrew.com) - primary
- Mobile web (responsive) - growing
- University partnerships - enterprise
- Social media - awareness
- Word-of-mouth - referrals

**Accessibility**:
- Works on any device (cloud-based)
- No installation required
- Korean + English support

### 3.4 Promotion

**Integrated Marketing Communications (IMC)**:
- Digital marketing (Meta Ads, Google Ads)
- Content marketing (blog, YouTube)
- Social media (Instagram, LinkedIn)
- Events (hackathons, workshops)
- PR (press releases, media coverage)
- Influencer partnerships (student leaders, YouTubers)

## 4. Customer Journey & Marketing Tactics

### 4.1 Awareness Stage

**Goal**: Make target audience aware of WKU Software Crew

**Tactics**:

1. **Social Media Campaigns**:
   - Instagram ads targeting university students (18-25, Jeollabuk-do)
   - Facebook campus groups (Wonkwang University)
   - LinkedIn posts (professional tone for B2B)
   - TikTok short videos (fun, educational)

2. **Content Marketing**:
   - Blog posts (SEO-optimized): "10 Best Free Coding Platforms for Korean Students"
   - YouTube channel: Coding tutorials, student success stories
   - Podcast: Interviews with student entrepreneurs

3. **Campus Marketing**:
   - Posters in CS department
   - Flyers in cafeteria
   - Banner ads on university website (sponsorship)
   - Presentations in CS classes (with professor permission)

4. **PR**:
   - Press release: "Wonkwang Students Launch EdTech Startup"
   - Media outreach: Local newspapers, tech blogs (Platum, GeekNews)
   - Guest posts on university blogs

**Budget**: ₩10M (Awareness campaigns)

**KPIs**: Website visits (10,000/month), Social media followers (2,000), Brand awareness survey (30% in target market)

### 4.2 Consideration Stage

**Goal**: Convince users that WKU Software Crew is the best choice

**Tactics**:

1. **Free Trial**:
   - 7-day free trial of Premium features (no credit card required)
   - Highlighted on homepage with prominent CTA

2. **Social Proof**:
   - User testimonials on website
   - Case studies (e.g., "How Minji landed her first internship")
   - Student success stories (video format)
   - Star ratings and reviews

3. **Comparison Content**:
   - "WKU Software Crew vs. Elice vs. Inflearn" comparison table
   - Highlight differentiators (all-in-one, startup support)

4. **Email Nurturing**:
   - Welcome email series (5 emails over 2 weeks)
   - Educational content (tips for learning to code)
   - Feature highlights (IDE, deployment)
   - Success stories

**Budget**: ₩5M (Content creation, email marketing tools)

**KPIs**: Sign-up rate (5% of website visitors), Email open rate (30%), Click-through rate (5%)

### 4.3 Conversion Stage

**Goal**: Convert free users to paying customers

**Tactics**:

1. **In-App Prompts**:
   - "Upgrade to Premium" banners (non-intrusive)
   - Feature paywalls with clear benefits
   - Limited-time offers (20% off for first month)

2. **Upgrade Campaigns**:
   - Email: "Unlock Senior Class with Premium" (after completing Junior Class)
   - Push notifications (if PWA): "Your free trial ends in 3 days"

3. **Incentives**:
   - Annual plan discount (save 2 months)
   - "Upgrade now and get 1 month free"
   - Referral bonus (invite friends, get discounts)

4. **Sales Demos** (B2B):
   - Personalized demos for universities
   - Free pilot program (50 students, 3 months)
   - ROI calculator (show cost savings vs. competitors)

**Budget**: ₩5M (Conversion optimization, A/B testing tools)

**KPIs**: Free-to-paid conversion rate (10%), Average deal size (B2B: ₩30M)

### 4.4 Retention Stage

**Goal**: Keep users engaged and reduce churn

**Tactics**:

1. **Engagement Features**:
   - Weekly learning reports (email)
   - Gamification (badges, streaks, leaderboard)
   - Community events (monthly hackathon)

2. **Customer Success**:
   - Proactive outreach to inactive users
   - "We miss you" emails
   - Personalized learning recommendations

3. **New Features**:
   - Regular feature releases (monthly)
   - "What's New" announcements

4. **Surveys**:
   - NPS surveys (quarterly)
   - Feature request voting

**Budget**: ₩5M (Email marketing, community management tools)

**KPIs**: Week 4 retention (> 30%), Monthly churn rate (< 5%), NPS (> 70)

### 4.5 Advocacy Stage

**Goal**: Turn happy users into brand advocates

**Tactics**:

1. **Referral Program**:
   - "Invite a friend, both get 1 month free"
   - Shareable referral links
   - Leaderboard for top referrers

2. **Ambassador Program**:
   - Select 10 passionate users as ambassadors
   - Give them free Pro accounts
   - Ask them to create content (blog posts, videos)
   - Feature them on website

3. **User-Generated Content**:
   - Encourage project showcases
   - "Student of the Month" spotlight
   - Share success stories on social media

4. **Testimonials**:
   - Request testimonials from happy users
   - Video testimonials (more authentic)
   - LinkedIn recommendations

**Budget**: ₩5M (Referral rewards, ambassador perks)

**KPIs**: K-Factor (> 0.5), Referral sign-ups (20% of total), User-generated content (50 projects/month)

## 5. Channel Strategy

### 5.1 Owned Channels

#### Website (wkucrew.com)

**Purpose**: Primary conversion funnel

**Key Pages**:
- Homepage (hero, features, social proof, CTA)
- Courses page (catalog with filters)
- Pricing page (3 tiers, FAQs)
- About page (team, mission)
- Blog (SEO content)
- Login/Sign-up

**SEO Strategy**:
- Target keywords: "온라인 코딩 플랫폼", "무료 프로그래밍 강의", "클라우드 IDE"
- Blog posts (2-3/week)
- Backlinks from university websites
- Fast load times (< 2s)

**CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization)**:
- A/B test CTAs, headlines, pricing
- Heatmaps (Hotjar)
- Exit-intent popups (discount offers)

#### Email Marketing

**List Segments**:
- Free users (nurturing)
- Premium users (engagement)
- Pro users (upsell, referrals)
- Churned users (win-back)

**Campaigns**:
- Welcome series (5 emails)
- Weekly newsletter (tips, new courses)
- Product updates
- Promotional offers

**Tools**: Mailchimp or Sendgrid

#### Social Media

**Instagram** (@wkucrew):
- Target: Students (18-25)
- Content: Student spotlights, coding tips, behind-the-scenes
- Posting frequency: 3-5 times/week
- Stories: Daily

**LinkedIn** (WKU Software Crew):
- Target: B2B (universities, corporations)
- Content: Thought leadership, case studies, company updates
- Posting frequency: 2-3 times/week

**YouTube** (WKU Software Crew):
- Content: Free coding tutorials, student success stories, platform demos
- Upload frequency: 1-2 videos/week
- Optimize for SEO (titles, descriptions, tags)

### 5.2 Paid Channels

#### Meta Ads (Facebook + Instagram)

**Budget**: ₩10M (Year 1)

**Campaigns**:
- Awareness: Video ads showcasing platform
- Consideration: Carousel ads (feature highlights)
- Conversion: Sign-up campaigns with lead forms

**Targeting**:
- Age: 18-25
- Location: Jeollabuk-do (later expand)
- Interests: Programming, education, startups
- Lookalike audiences (from email list)

**Creative**:
- Student testimonial videos
- "Learn to code for free" graphics
- Platform demo GIFs

#### Google Ads

**Budget**: ₩10M (Year 1)

**Search Ads**:
- Target keywords: "온라인 코딩", "무료 프로그래밍", "클라우드 IDE"
- Bid on competitor keywords (Elice, Inflearn)
- Landing pages optimized for each keyword

**Display Ads**:
- Remarketing (users who visited but didn't sign up)
- Placement: Tech blogs, university websites

**YouTube Ads**:
- TrueView ads (skippable)
- Target: Users watching coding tutorials

### 5.3 Earned Channels

#### PR & Media Coverage

**Goals**:
- 5+ media mentions (Year 1)
- 1+ feature in major tech blog (Platum, TechCrunch Korea)

**Strategy**:
- Press releases (product launch, funding rounds)
- Media kits (logo, screenshots, fact sheet)
- Journalist outreach (personalized emails)
- Story angles: "University students disrupting EdTech", "Making coding accessible"

#### Influencer Partnerships

**Micro-influencers** (10K-100K followers):
- Tech YouTubers (coding tutorial creators)
- University student influencers
- Developer influencers on LinkedIn/Twitter

**Partnership Models**:
- Sponsored content (₩500K-₩2M per video)
- Affiliate program (20% commission on referrals)
- Free Pro accounts (for reviews)

**Budget**: ₩10M (Year 1)

#### Word of Mouth

**Strategies**:
- Referral program (incentivized)
- Exceptional product experience
- Community events (in-person meetups)
- University ambassadors

## 8. Competitive Analysis & Positioning

### 8.1 Competitor Comparison

| Competitor | Strengths | Weaknesses | Our Advantage |
|------------|-----------|------------|---------------|
| **Elice** | Established, university partnerships | Expensive (₩50M+/year for B2B) | Cheaper (₩10M-₩50M), student-focused |
| **Inflearn** | Large course library | No IDE, no startup support | All-in-one platform |
| **Programmers** | Job board integration | Korean companies only | Startup support, international |
| **Codecademy** | Global brand | Expensive, English only | Affordable, Korean support |

### 8.2 Blue Ocean Strategy

**Eliminate**:
- Expensive enterprise sales
- Physical office requirements
- Outdated curriculum

**Reduce**:
- Pricing (vs. competitors)
- Setup complexity

**Raise**:
- Hands-on projects
- Community support
- Accessibility (free tier)

**Create**:
- All-in-one platform (LMS + IDE + Deployment)
- Startup incubation program
- University-specific features
